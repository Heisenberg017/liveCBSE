Classroom Coaching Classic CBSE XI (Science)

Course Briefing.

The course has been designed for the the students who want to achieve high percentages in Class XI Exams. The classroom program is also beneficial for the students, who are preparing for the competitive examinations but, at the same time, want to keep an eye on the preparation for board exam.
The Course Highlights are:

Daily Assignments

  • Lecture Supplements
  • Home Assignments

Modular/Full Tests
The students can take tests in three formats:

  • Topic Specific Tests
  • Modular Tests (based on a group of topics)
  • Full Tests

Course Material

  • Complete NCERT covered
  • Separate section for Proofs and Derivations

The methodology of teaching at domain E includes:

Lectures

Lectures at domain E will be targeting complete syllabus of Class XI CBSE Board Examination based on the NCERT Textbooks. The students will also be made to learn how to present the Proofs and Derivations required for the final CBSE Examination.

Assignments

Lecture Supplements: The solved examples to be attempted by the students are a part of Lecture Supplements.
Home Assignment: Along with Lecture Supplements few selected problems will be given to the students as home assignment.

Course Material
The students would be given tutorial sheets at the end of each topic. These contain:

  • Complete Theory
  • Write the doubt just the topic as well as subtopic or in a sentence format for E.g. I have doubt in Rotational Motion and in the specific topic rolling.
  • Lots of solved examples
  • Complete NCERT problems covered.
  • Separate section for Proofs and Derivations.

Periodic Tests

Tests will be conducted periodically on each topic or a group of topics. These tests are important for the students for understanding the pattern of the exam. It will also help the students being in touch with CBSE preparation throughout the year.
The students can take tests in three formats:

  • Topic Specific Tests
  • Modular Tests (based on a group of topics)
  • Full Tests (tests on full course)
